Reflection

Lessons learned from my academic journey and professional experiences that have shaped my career and growth.

Lessons from School

While pursuing my Bachelor’s Degree in Mechanical Engineering at Arizona State University (ASU), I’ve gained valuable insights that have enhanced my technical and professional skills:

  • Problem-Solving Through Engineering Principles: Courses in mechanics and thermodynamics taught me how to break down complex problems into manageable parts, a skill I now apply to both academic and real-world challenges.
  • Time Management and Discipline: Balancing coursework with my professional responsibilities has honed my ability to prioritize tasks and meet deadlines, a critical skill in high-pressure environments.
  • Collaboration and Teamwork: Group projects at ASU emphasized the importance of effective communication and collaboration, preparing me to work efficiently with diverse teams in my career.

Lessons from Previous Jobs

My 20+ years of experience in construction, logistics, and operations management have provided me with practical lessons that continue to guide my professional journey:

  • Leadership Under Pressure (CB Construction): As the owner of CB Construction, I managed large-scale projects and teams of up to 30 employees. Leading disaster recovery efforts after Hurricane Katrina taught me how to remain calm and make strategic decisions in high-stress situations.
  • Safety as a Priority (Green Metals Inc.): Serving as Safety Committee President for two years reinforced the importance of proactive safety measures. I learned how to implement and enforce safety protocols, reducing risks and fostering a culture of accountability.
  • Efficiency Through Process Improvement (Futaba): Working as a Stamping Operator at Futaba, I applied Toyota’s Kaizen and 5S methodologies to streamline operations, a practice I now use to optimize workflows in any role.
  • Adaptability and Resilience (ISA): Operating heavy machinery and managing scrap yard logistics at ISA taught me how to adapt to changing conditions and maintain efficiency, even in challenging environments.
